Johor Fish Curry

Description:

Yellow Curry Fish is a delightful and flavorful dish that brings together the richness of coconut milk, the aromatic blend of yellow curry paste, and the succulence of white fish fillets. This Thai-inspired culinary creation combines a medley of vibrant vegetables, including carrots, zucchini, and butternut pumpkin, to create a harmonious and visually appealing ensemble.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp peanut oil
  • 1 brown onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, thinly sliced
  • 1 zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 400g butternut pumpkin, cut into 2cm pieces
  • 2 tbsp yellow curry paste
  • 400ml can coconut milk
  • 700g firm white fish fillets (such as blue grenadier), cut into 3cm pieces
  • 2 x 250g pkts Tilda Steamed Brown Basmati Rice
  • 1/2 cup coriander leaves
  • 1 tbsp fried shallots

Steps:

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of peanut oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the finely chopped brown onion to the pan and saut√© until it becomes translucent.
  3. Next, add the thinly sliced carrot and zucchini to the pan, cooking for a few minutes until they start to soften.
  4. Introduce the 2cm pieces of butternut pumpkin to the pan and continue cooking, allowing them to absorb the flavors.
  5. Stir in 2 tablespoons of yellow curry paste, coating the vegetables evenly with the aromatic curry paste.
  6. Pour in the 400ml can of coconut milk, creating a creamy base for the curry.
  7. Add the 700g of firm white fish fillets, cut into 3cm pieces, and gently simmer until the fish is tender and cooked through.
  8. While the curry is simmering, prepare the Tilda Steamed Brown Basmati Rice according to the package instructions.
  9. Serve the Yellow Curry Fish over the cooked brown basmati rice, garnishing with fresh coriander leaves and a sprinkle of fried shallots.
  10. Enjoy your homemade Yellow Curry Fish!
